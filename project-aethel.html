Case Study: Aethel High-Fashion
AETHEL

Project Overview: AETHEL

Role: UI/UX Designer.

Objective: To design a high-end fashion e-commerce application that prioritizes brand curation and a premium, editorial shopping experience.

Design Philosophy: Focused on a "soft luxury" aesthetic, utilizing a minimalist layout, muted color palettes, and high-contrast typography to emphasize exclusivity and class.

1. Challenge & Goals

Challenge: High-fashion e-commerce platforms often prioritize quantity over quality, leading to overwhelming interfaces that detract from the exclusivity of the brand.

Goals: To create a 'soft luxury' mobile experience where the visual storytelling remains the primary focus. The goal was to build a cohesive visual system that bridges the gap between editorial-style browsing and efficient, high-end mobile commerce.

2. Market Analysis

I analyzed existing luxury fashion apps to identify common pitfalls in mobile layout design. My focus was on how top-tier brands balance dense product grids with white space, and how they handle CTA placement to maintain a premium feel without sacrificing utility.

Key Findings:

A. The "Soft Luxury" Gap: There is an opportunity for a platform that balances a refined, minimalist aesthetic with intuitive mobile-first patterns, specifically by using whitespace and controlled typography to elevate the perceived value of items.

B. The "Soft Luxury" Gap: There is an opportunity for a platform that balances a refined, minimalist aesthetic with intuitive mobile-first patterns, specifically by using whitespace and controlled typography to elevate the perceived value of items.

C. Inconsistent Visual Hierarchy: Competitors frequently struggle to balance editorial content with functional navigation, leading to a fragmented user experience where the brand identity feels disconnected from the shopping utility.
